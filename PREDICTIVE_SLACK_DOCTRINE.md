# Predictive Slack Doctrine

Once SIMD turns authority updates from “expensive Actor work” into **cheap byte-field work**, the remaining CPU cycles become a new resource:
**Not spare time for more detail, but spare time for future-state computation.**

The law is strict:
**Prediction has no standing.**
Prediction may prepare, prefetch, warn, rank, render a ghost, or propose.
Only observation + admission + receipt creates truth.

The state classes become:
`CURRENT_ADMITTED, PREDICTED, PREFETCHED, SHADOW, CANDIDATE, REFUSED, REPLAYED`

Prediction is **slack-cycle option manufacturing**. ⚙️

---

## 1. The Math of Spare Cycles
If one SIMD authority pass over the active world costs 0.5 ms, then 3 ms of slack frame budget can buy 6 additional future passes (t+1 to t+6).
The system continuously asks:
- What is about to fail?
- What is about to become visible?
- What is about to become CROWN?
- What should be prefetched/demoted/refused?

## 2. Phase Change: From Reactive to Anticipatory World
Old model: Player acts → World reacts → Renderer updates.
New SIMD model: World computes possible near futures → Semantic LOD ranks relevance → UE4 prepares projection → Player experiences continuity.
Future candidates are built in shadow, then admitted only if reality takes that path.

---

## 3. What can be done in the gaps?

### A. Future Semantic LOD
The world focuses ahead of attention. A BACKGROUND object predicted to crash in 0.8 seconds is promoted to PRIMARY before impact. UE4 preloads better materials and collision.

### B. Predictive Geometry Warming
Speculative render manufacturing. Prepare cracked meshes, warning sounds, and particle pools before failure threshold is crossed. Discard if failure does not occur.

### C. Failure Precursor Mechanics
Instead of sudden failure, compute precursor cones (heat rising, stress accumulating). Skilled players read the future before casual players see the event.

### D. Consequence Cones
Every action produces a cone of consequences with probability risks and visual warnings. The UI becomes an engineering forecast surface.

### E. Shadow Worlds
Compute shadow futures (t+100ms, t+1s). Compare current vs future to extract high-risk deltas and future CROWN promotions.

### F. Worldline Vectorization
Compute counterfactual futures cheaply. Driver-assist forecasts, pit strategy projections, factory throughput alternatives.

## 4. Mechanics That Become Possible
1. **Engineer Vision:** Toggle overlays showing time-to-failure and future stress states.
2. **Predictive Racing Line:** Line shows evolving future grip and tire heat.
3. **Pre-Failure Mech Combat:** Attack the future (force a heat spike) rather than the present (deal damage).
4. **Predictive Repair:** Maintenance becomes strategic forecasting.
5. **Agent Jidoka as Gameplay:** Predict factory defects and choose to stop the line or risk bad batches.
6. **Predictive Crowd Attention:** Crowds react to predicted trajectories before impact.
7. **Living Market Forecast:** Trade on predicted factory output drops and supply chain delays.
8. **Predictive Asset Streaming:** The world feels dense because density arrives before attention.
9. **Semantic Ambush Surfaces:** AI and players read future danger/cover maps.
10. **Counterfactual Replay:** Forensics mechanic showing actual vs avoided paths.

---

## 5. The "Never Considered" Mechanic Class
**Receipted Counterfactual Maintenance**
The game continuously predicts mission-capability futures. The player sees warnings, runs inspections, chooses interventions, and later receives receipts showing whether the intervention actually preserved capability. This is lifecycle systems engineering as a game.

## 6. Architecture: Predictive Slack Engine

```text
Current Authority State
    ↓
SIMD Transition Law
    ↓
Shadow Future Buffers
    ↓
Risk / Relevance / Entropy Scans
    ↓
Semantic LOD Forecast
    ↓
Projection Prefetch Queue
    ↓
Verifier / Admission Gate
    ↓
Receipt if realized
```

Critical exclusion: **Predicted state must never overwrite admitted state.**

## Final Law
SIMD gives us cheap present-state authority.
Slack cycles give us cheap future-state candidates.
Semantic LOD decides which futures matter.
Agent Jidoka prevents prediction from pretending to be truth.
Receipts convert realized futures into standing.

**The spare cycles are not for more polygons. They are for manufacturing the future before it becomes the present.**
