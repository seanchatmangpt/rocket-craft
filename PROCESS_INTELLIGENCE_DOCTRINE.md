# Process Intelligence Doctrine

From a van der Aalst process-mining lens, the rule is:
**Do not make process intelligence a dashboard. Make process intelligence the game mechanic.**

Process mining discovers, monitors, and improves real processes from event logs. In Rocket-Craft / GMF / Eden, this translates to:
- event logs → world memory
- process models → mission maps
- conformance → skill/style/challenge
- deviations → drama
- bottlenecks → tension
- alignments → ghost paths
- OCEL → multi-object causality
- process improvement → gameplay progression

---

## 1. Process Intelligence as a Game Loop
Enterprise asks: What actually happened? How does that compare to the model? Where are bottlenecks?
Game loop: Act → log → mine → reveal → diagnose → improve → replay → attempt again.
**Process mining as roguelike replay intelligence.**

## 2. Discovery: The World Reveals Its Real Rules
The game does not tell you the full system upfront. You discover the process by playing.
The factory draws its own dungeon map from your mistakes.

## 3. Conformance: Skill, Style, and Tension
The intended process is the ideal line. The event log is what actually happened. The fun is in the gap.
Deviations can be mistakes, exploits, shortcuts, sabotage, or innovations.
**Player-discovered process evolution.**

## 4. Alignments: Ghost Paths
Show the player their actual trace overlaid against the ideal trace.
Green ghost = intended process
Red ghost = actual deviation
Yellow ghost = predicted consequence
Blue ghost = optimized alternative

## 5. Bottlenecks Become Enemies
The boss fight is not a monster. The boss fight is a process bottleneck becoming physical.
Queue buildups, parts starvation, delayed mechs. The enemy is the process.

## 6. OCEL: Multi-Object Causality
A single event touches multiple objects.
"This weapon socket came from Batch 17. Batch 17 skipped inspection. It failed during Lap 4. Its failure changed the market price."
OCEL turns the world into a web of consequences.

## 7. Process Mining as Post-Run Forensics
After a race or factory run, the player gets a cinematic forensic replay: timeline, ghost paths, heat maps, failure cones, receipt chain.
"Your mech failed because of a cooling line delay 5 steps ago."
Detective game inside a racing/mech game.

## 8. Process Improvement Becomes Progression
Unlocks are not just better stats; they are a smarter process (better inspection gates, faster bottleneck detection, improved pit choreography).
**Upgrade the capability system, not just the gear.**

## 9. Fun Features List
- **Process Ghost Replay:** Colored trails showing intended vs actual paths.
- **Bottleneck Bosses:** Physical manifestations of jams and delays.
- **Deviation Combos:** Rewarding productive deviations (innovations).
- **Andon Events:** Jidoka as gameplay—diagnosing predicted process failures.
- **Causal Loot:** Parts carry process provenance and history.
- **Process Reputation:** Earn reputation via process reliability, not just faction points.
- **Supplier Mystery:** Supply-chain detective gameplay based on batch failures.
- **Living Tutorials:** Adaptive tutorials mined from player behavior patterns.
- **Strategy Forecast Board:** Choose strategies based on forecast bottlenecks and failure cones.
- **Process-Building as Base Building:** Designing workflows and inspection routes instead of just placing buildings.

## 10. The Core Game Design Law
Do not show process intelligence as a dashboard, KPI table, or compliance report.
Show it as ghosts, heat maps, warnings, replays, causal trails, future cones, bottleneck monsters, and upgradable processes.
**"I understand the machine better now." That feeling is fun.**

## 11. The Crown Feature
**Process Forensics Replay**
Every failure has a story. Every story has evidence. Every improvement changes the next run.

---
**Final Law: Do not gamify process mining. Use process mining to make the game world remember, explain, predict, and improve itself.**
