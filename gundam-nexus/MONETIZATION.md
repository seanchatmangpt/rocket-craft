# Gundam Nexus — Monetization Strategy
## Comprehensive Revenue Architecture and 5-Year Projection

**Document Type:** Business/Revenue Design  
**Scope:** All monetization systems, pricing structures, compliance frameworks, and projections  
**Target:** $2.5B cumulative 5-year revenue across 5 revenue engines  
**Guiding Principle:** Player-first monetization — no pay-to-win, no predatory mechanics, full regulatory compliance globally

---

## Executive Summary

Gundam Nexus monetizes exclusively through cosmetic and convenience systems. Mobile suits — the core gameplay units — are earned through play or purchased at flat prices, never locked behind gacha. The five revenue engines below operate in parallel, each targeting a different segment of the player base and spending behavior. Conservative projections reach $2.5B over five years; the upside case (assuming Bandai partnership and esports growth) exceeds $3.2B.

The model is explicitly designed to comply with Belgium, Netherlands, South Korea, and prospective UK loot box regulations. All randomized elements contain exclusively cosmetic items. Spending caps, transparent odds, and parental controls are mandatory, not optional.

## Revenue Engine 1: Cosmetic Gacha

**Annual Revenue Potential: $200–300M**

### Overview

The gacha system in Gundam Nexus targets the collector segment of the player base — fans who want their favorite suit variants, pilot looks, and battle effects to reflect their personal taste and series loyalty. Critically, every gacha item is cosmetic. No gacha pull ever affects match outcome.

### Pull Rate Structure

All pull rates are displayed on every banner screen before the player commits currency. Rates are also published in the in-game "Probability Archive" accessible from the main menu at any time.

| Rarity | Label | Pull Rate | Description |
|--------|-------|-----------|-------------|
| SSR | Gunpla Master | 3% | Legendary animated suit skins, canon collab pilot suits |
| SR | Ace Pilot | 12% | High-detail paint variants, signature pilot suits, special beam colors |
| R | Recruit | 85% | Standard recolor schemes, generic emblems, common cockpit decals |

These rates comply with current regulations in Belgium, the Netherlands, Japan, China (published odds requirement), South Korea, and Australia. The system is designed for the most restrictive anticipated regulatory regime globally, meaning future tightening in any major market requires no structural changes.

### Banner Structure

**Limited Banner (14-Day Duration)**
- Features one SSR suit cosmetic at increased rate (3% → 4.5% for featured item)
- Features two SR variants on rate-up (12% → 18% split between both)
- Available for purchase window only; returns to "limited vault" after expiry
- Maximum one active limited banner at any time (prevents player spending fatigue and FOMO exhaustion)
- Announced 7 days in advance with full preview of all possible pulls

**Standard Banner (Permanent)**
- Always available
- Full pull rate table applies (3% / 12% / 85%)
- Contains all non-limited SSR items that have "graduated" from limited banners (90-day delay after limited window closes)
- No rate-up; pure random across the full pool
- Recommended for free-to-play players accumulating the pity system

**Collab Banner (7-Day Duration)**
- Activated during cross-IP collaboration events (see Revenue Engine 4)
- Collab-exclusive cosmetics that do not graduate to standard banner
- Higher SSR rate during collab window (3% → 5%) to respect the limited time
- Partner IP art style elements applied to Gundam suits (e.g., Code Geass paintwork on the Sinanju)
- Hard limit: one collab banner active at a time

### Pity System

The pity system is fully transparent, displayed in real time on every banner screen, and persists across sessions (never resets on log-out).

**Soft Pity (Begins at Pull 70)**
- Pulls 70–89 on any single banner increase SSR rate by +2% per pull above 70
- At pull 70: SSR rate = 5%. At pull 75: 15%. At pull 79: 23%
- This is communicated as a visible progress bar labeled "Resonance Building"

**Hard Pity (Pull 90 — Guaranteed)**
- Pull 90 is mathematically guaranteed to produce an SSR item
- If the banner features a rate-up SSR, there is a 50/50 chance the hard pity pull produces the featured item
- If the 50/50 fails, the next SSR pull (which may occur at any point including the very next pull) is guaranteed to be the featured item
- This "guaranteed featured" status persists across banners for the same banner type (limited pity carries to the next limited banner)
- Hard pity counter resets to zero after any SSR pull (soft pity counter resets at the same time)

**Pity Inheritance Rules**
- Limited banner pity is shared across all limited banners
- Standard banner has its own independent pity counter
- Collab banners have their own independent pity counter
- Players can view all three pity counters simultaneously from the "Nexus Archive" inventory screen

### What Is Gachable

The gacha system contains exclusively cosmetic items. These are enumerated below:

**Suit Cosmetics**
- Color schemes and paint variants (e.g., "Char's Custom Red" applied to the RX-78-2, a non-canonical what-if scheme)
- Weathering effects (battle-worn, pristine showroom, space-exposure aged)
- Thruster trail color variants (standard blue, custom gold, psycho-frame pink, Trans-Am red)
- Shield decal sets (12 decals per set, applied to shoulder armor and shields)
- Verniers and fin detail highlight colors

**Pilot Cosmetics**
- Pilot suit variants (Normal Suit variants from UC, Calamity War-era suits from IBO, Phantom Pain uniforms from SEED)
- Helmet visor color (tinted, clear, reflective)
- "Expression sets" — pilot idle animation variants visible during pre-match lobby

**Battle Effects**
- Beam saber color variants (standard green, custom gold, Alaya-Vijnana orange-red, Newtype gold)
- Beam rifle shot color and trail effect
- Explosion particle color variants (standard orange, SEED pink, psycho-frame red-white)
- "Impact seal" — a custom emblem that flashes on a perfect parry hit

**Cockpit Decorations**
- Dashboard ornament models (visible in cockpit-cam kill cams)
- Instrument panel color scheme
- Background display art (visible during pilot cutscenes)

**Animated Emblems**
- Player card emblems that loop a short animation in lobby and post-match screens
- Series-specific animated emblems (e.g., the ZERO System activation animation, a Unicorn NT-D transformation thumbnail)

### What Is NOT Gachable

The following items are strictly outside the gacha economy. They are acquired through gameplay or flat-price purchases only.

- **Mobile suits themselves** — all suits are either earned through 200+ hours of play, direct purchase at flat price ($4.99–$14.99 per suit), or the Gunpla AR Bridge system
- **Stat upgrades or modifications** — no gacha item ever changes a suit's numerical statistics
- **Gameplay mechanics or systems** — no gacha pull unlocks a game feature, mode, or mechanical capability
- **Story content** — all narrative chapters, campaign missions, and lore entries are free or included with base game
- **Ranked match access** — no paywall on competitive modes

### Price Anchor Structure

Credits are the premium currency used for gacha banners, battle pass purchases, and cosmetic shop items.

| Bundle | Credits | Price (USD) | Effective Rate | Bonus |
|--------|---------|-------------|----------------|-------|
| Starter Pack | 680 | $9.99 | 68/$ | None (first purchase only; includes 1 guaranteed SR) |
| Regular Pack | 600 | $9.99 | 60/$ | None |
| Mid Pack | 1640 | $24.99 | 65.6/$ | 8.5% bonus credits |
| Large Pack | 3280 | $49.99 | 65.6/$ | 9.3% bonus credits |
| Premium Pack | 6480 | $99.99 | 64.8/$ | 8% bonus credits |
| Elite Pack | 12960 | $199.99 | 64.8/$ | 8% bonus credits |

One banner pull costs 160 Credits ($2.67 effective). Ten-pull costs 1600 Credits ($26.67 effective). With the hard pity guarantee at pull 90, the maximum cost to guarantee an SSR is 90 × 160 = 14,400 Credits (~$240 at worst-case rate).

**Monthly Spending Cap:** $500 per account per calendar month. This cap is enforced at the payment processor level, not just the UI level. Parents can set lower caps via Family Mode (minimum $0, maximum $500) using a PIN.

### Per-Banner Revenue Model

The following model applies to a single major limited banner:

- Daily Active Users (DAU): 3,000,000
- Spenders (15% of DAU): 450,000
- Average Revenue Per Paying User (ARPPU) per banner: $25
- Banner duration: 14 days
- Per-banner gross revenue: 450,000 × $25 = **$11.25M**

At a cadence of 24 banners per year (approximately 2 per month, accounting for collab banners):
- Annual gacha revenue: 24 × $11.25M = **$270M**

This figure assumes no banner-to-banner variance (real deployments will see major IP collabs and anniversary banners exceeding $20M and quieter maintenance banners reaching $5M). The $270M annual estimate is considered the mid-case.

**Conservative case** (1.5M DAU, 10% spend rate, $20 ARPPU): $180M/year  
**Upside case** (5M DAU, 20% spend rate, $30 ARPPU): $360M/year

## Revenue Engine 2: Seasonal Battle Pass

**Annual Revenue Potential: $120–180M**

### Overview

The Seasonal Battle Pass targets the core engaged player who logs in regularly but may not spend on gacha. The pass rewards consistent play rather than spending volume, making it the most ethically straightforward monetization vector.

### Pass Structure

**Free Track (10 Rewards)**
All players who log in and complete basic daily missions access the free track. Rewards are meaningful but introductory.

- Rewards include: 200–400 Credits, common cosmetic emblems, one R-rarity suit recolor scheme, crafting materials, and one free "Gunpla Frame" (used in Build Fighters system)
- The free track is designed so non-paying players feel rewarded, not excluded

**Premium Track (40 Rewards) — $9.99/Month**
The premium battle pass unlocks 40 additional reward tiers stacked on top of the free track.

- 1 Legendary suit cosmetic (exclusive animated skin, never in gacha during the active season)
- 3 pilot suit variants (series-specific; each season features a different Gundam series)
- 5 seasonal emblems (static and animated options)
- 400 Credits (partially recoups pass cost for dedicated players)
- Seasonal "Chapter Frame" — a unique cockpit decoration available only that season
- "Suit of the Season" voter badge — grants a vote in the community season poll (see below)

**Season Length:** 90 days (4 seasons per year, aligned with calendar quarters)

**Premium Pass Plus — $14.99/Month**
For players who want accelerated progress:
- All Premium Track rewards
- 10 instant tier-skips (skip missions, jump reward tiers)
- "Season Log" digital artbook — high-resolution artwork from that season's featured series
- Priority queue during peak hours (aesthetic indicator only; no gameplay effect)

### Suit of the Season — Community Vote

Each season, a shortlist of 5 canonical Gundam suits is assembled by the art team. Premium pass holders vote on which suit receives the season's "Legendary Cosmetic" treatment — an animated skin with custom effects.

- Voting opens at the midpoint of the preceding season
- Results are announced via livestream event
- The winning suit's skin is revealed with a 3-minute animated reveal trailer
- Previous season winners are archived; non-season players can purchase past Suit of Season skins at 2,400 Credits after a 6-month exclusivity window

This system creates organic community engagement, social media momentum, and a sense of player agency over the content roadmap.

### Engagement Hook Design

The pass is designed around three temporal layers of engagement, each calibrated to realistic session lengths for a mobile mecha game:

**Daily Missions (5-Minute Target)**
- 3 missions per day
- Examples: "Win 1 match," "Deal 1,000 damage with beam weapons," "Perform 3 perfect parries"
- Reward: Pass XP (fills tier progress bar)
- Hard cap: 3 completable daily missions maximum to prevent unhealthy session extensions

**Weekly Challenges (30-Minute Target)**
- 1 major challenge per week
- Examples: "Win 5 ranked matches using a SEED-era suit," "Activate Trans-Am 10 times in a single week," "Reach a Duel Ranking milestone"
- Reward: Pass XP equivalent to 4 daily missions + 50–100 Credits
- Cannot be completed by spending Credits — play only

**Seasonal Narrative Chapters**
- 6 short narrative chapters released across the 90-day season
- Each chapter is a 15-minute visual novel interlude starring that season's featured series
- Unlocks at pass tiers 5, 10, 20, 30, 40, and final tier
- Non-interactive (visual novel format) so they're accessible to all player types and localized fully

### Revenue Model

| Variable | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Total registered players | 10,000,000 |
| Active per season | 3,000,000 |
| Pass purchase rate | 30% |
| Paying pass players per season | 900,000 |
| Revenue per player (Premium) | $9.99 × 3 months = $29.97 |
| Revenue per season | ~$27M |
| Revenue per year (4 seasons) | **$108M** |

Plus Pass Plus uplifts approximately 20% of Premium purchasers to $14.99/month:
- 180,000 × $4 additional × 3 months = $2.16M per season = $8.6M/year additional
- **Total adjusted annual revenue: ~$116M**

Upside: If pass purchase rate rises to 40% (achievable with strong season content), annual revenue reaches $155M.

## Revenue Engine 3: Gunpla AR Bridge

**Annual Revenue Potential: $150–200M**

### Overview

The Gunpla AR Bridge is the most novel and highest-upside revenue engine in the Nexus model. It creates a direct economic loop between the physical model kit market (which Bandai sells ~$800M/year of globally) and the Gundam Nexus digital ecosystem.

This is not a gimmick. The Digimon franchise demonstrated with physical toy-to-digital bonds a decade ago that physical-digital integration drives both segments. Gundam Build Fighters and Build Divers have demonstrated that in-franchise media specifically designed around kit building significantly boosts kit sales. The AR Bridge is the commercial monetization of that flywheel.

### How It Works

1. Player purchases a physical Bandai Gunpla kit from any authorized retailer (or online)
2. Inside the box (or on the box exterior for DE-marked kits): a QR code or NFC-readable tag
3. Player scans the QR code using the Gundam Nexus app camera (or taps NFC-enabled phone to NFC chip in PG Unleashed boxes)
4. The Nexus backend validates the kit serial number against Bandai's kit registry API
5. Player receives the "Digital Edition" of that exact suit as a playable unit in Gundam Nexus

The Digital Edition suit is not a cosmetic — it is a fully playable unit with its own stat base. This stat base is kit-tier-appropriate (see below) but designed to be competitive without being overpowered. The suit's appearance reflects the specific kit's design (e.g., MG 2.0 RX-78-2 has slightly different proportions than the HG RX-78-2).

### Kit Tier Pricing Model

| Kit Tier | Typical Retail (USD) | Digital Edition Premium | Total w/ DE | Digital Revenue (40% split) |
|----------|---------------------|------------------------|-------------|----------------------------|
| HG (High Grade) | $15–25 | +$10 | $25–35 | $4.00 |
| MG (Master Grade) | $30–60 | +$10 | $40–70 | $4.00 |
| RG (Real Grade) | $30–45 | +$10 | $40–55 | $4.00 |
| PG (Perfect Grade) | $70–100 | +$20 | $90–120 | $8.00 |
| PG Unleashed | $180–250 | +$50 | $230–300 | $20.00 |
| PG Unleashed (NFC) | $180–250 | included | $220–300 | $18.00 |

**Revenue split: 60% Bandai (physical/DE cost), 40% Nexus digital**

Bandai's incentive: the DE premium makes the kit a higher-margin product. A $15 HG kit sold at $25 is a 67% price increase; even at 60/40 split, Bandai nets $6 more per kit with DE vs. standard.

### What the Digital Edition Unlocks

- The exact suit as a new playable unit (slot added to player's hangar)
- Unique "Kit Model" appearance — distinct from in-game standard model, based on the physical kit's exact proportions and panel line design
- "Builder's Mark" emblem displayed on the suit's shoulder pauldron in-match
- Stat baseline: HG suits are "Recruit" class (solid stats, slight stat ceiling), MG suits are "Ace" class, PG suits are "Legend" class
- Legend-class suits have a special passive ability unique to that kit (e.g., PG Strike Freedom has "Wings of Light active" passive — fin funnels deploy automatically during Trans-Am)
- Kit bonus: Physical kit owners receive a cosmetic "Assembly Mode" toggle — suit displays panel line detail in-match that non-kit owners' version doesn't show

### Implementation Architecture

**Bandai Kit Registry API**
- REST API maintained by Bandai Digital (new division, existing infrastructure from Gundam Base app)
- Each kit serial number is a unique token minted at manufacturing
- Serial numbers are one-time-use (prevents resale of used codes)
- NFC chips in PG Unleashed boxes use encrypted serial format validated server-side
- API endpoint: `POST /api/nexus/validate` with body `{ "serial": "...", "kit_id": "...", "player_id": "..." }`
- Response includes: `suit_id`, `tier`, `stat_block`, `passive_ability_id`
- Rate limiting: 100 validations per player per year (prevents fraud at scale; legitimate collectors won't hit this)

**Fraud Prevention**
- Serial numbers are cryptographically signed at manufacturing
- Each serial number has a regional distribution flag; mismatched regions trigger a manual review
- Players flagged for anomalous validation patterns (more than 10 per week) are reviewed by trust and safety

**Digital-Only Backup**
- Players who cannot find or scan a kit code (gift, second-hand kit, code damaged) can contact support
- Support can manually validate with proof of purchase (receipt photo)
- Backdoor exists for Bandai promotional kits distributed at events (event-specific serial blocks)

### Revenue Projection

- Global annual Gunpla unit sales: ~8 million kits/year
- DE-marked kits as percentage of total: 30% (Bandai rollout; assumes DE becomes standard on new releases by Year 2)
- Annual DE-marked kits sold: 2.4 million
- Nexus player adoption rate among buyers: 25%
- Annual AR validations: 600,000
- Average digital premium per activation: $12 (weighted average across tiers)
- Annual digital revenue (40% share): 600,000 × $12 × 0.40 = **$28.8M Year 1**
- Year 2 (full DE rollout, 40% adoption): 2.4M units × 40% = 960,000 × $12 × 0.40 = **$46.1M**
- Year 3 (DE standard, 50% adoption): 4M units × 50% × $12 × 0.40 = **$96M**

The Year 3 figure assumes DE becomes standard on all new Bandai Gunpla globally (comparable to how QR codes became universal in product packaging by 2023). Physical brand events (Gundam Base Tokyo, Gundam Café) serve as additional DE adoption activation points.

### The Flywheel

Gundam Build Fighters (2013) drove a 40% spike in HG Gunpla sales during its broadcast. Build Divers Re:RISE sustained elevated kit sales throughout its run. The AR Bridge formalizes this relationship:

1. Digital engagement (players see a suit in Nexus) drives desire to own the physical kit
2. Physical kit purchase activates Digital Edition, which deepens digital engagement
3. Deeper digital engagement increases visibility of other suits, generating more kit desire
4. Bandai creates new kit releases timed to in-game features (e.g., new MG Barbatos Lupus Rex releases same week as IBO season event in Nexus)

This flywheel benefits both parties. Bandai has explicitly stated in investor materials that digital integration is a strategic priority for the Gunpla line post-2025.

## Revenue Engine 4: Cross-IP Collaboration Events

**Annual Revenue Potential: $80–150M**

### Overview

Collaboration events are among the most potent short-term revenue drivers in live service gaming. Fate/Grand Order's collaboration events with Type-Moon properties routinely generate $20–40M in two weeks. Gundam Nexus operates at 2–3x the franchise scale of FGO globally, making comparable figures achievable.

### Target IP Roster

**Tier 1: Sunrise / Bandai Namco Stablemates (Low Friction)**
- *Code Geass: Lelouch of the Rebellion* — Knightmare Frames, Suzaku Kururugi/Lelouch vi Britannia as pilot skins, "Geass Eye" beam effect. Zero's mask as cockpit decoration.
- *Macross* (various series) — Variable Fighter transformation poses as landing animation, Lynn Minmay microphone emblem, Itano Circus missile trail effect
- *The Big O* — Roger Smith and Dorothy pilot skins; Big O mech style applied to RX-78 as "Paradigm City" variant paint

**Tier 2: High-Value Non-Bandai IPs (Standard Licensing)**
- *Neon Genesis Evangelion / Rebuild* — Eva units reskinned as Gundam proportions, Rei/Shinji/Asuka pilot suits, AT Field visual effect on I-Field animation, "Angels" as enemy skins in collab story
- *Gunbuster / Diebuster* — Gainax mecha aesthetic applied to Nu Gundam; Coach Ohta's training regimen as a timed event challenge
- *SSSS.Gridman / Dynazenon* — SSSS.Gridman's grid-pattern beam effect; Alexis Kerib as villain cameo in collab chapter

**Tier 3: Stretch Collaborations (High Value, Complex Licensing)**
- *Transformers* — If Hasbro partnership achievable; Autobot/Decepticon emblems, Optimus Prime-colored RX-78 variant
- *Pacific Rim* — Jaeger-aesthetic Gundam repaints; Jaeger nameplate system applied to suit naming UI
- *Voltron / Golion* — Formation-style Fin Funnel animation; Voltron-colored beam saber set

### Event Structure

Each major collaboration event runs for 14 days. The structure is:

**Days 1–3: Announcement and Hype**
- In-game "signal interference" loading screen tease (partner IP's visual language bleeds into normal UI)
- Social media drops of single collab cosmetic per day
- Community speculation encouraged (no spoilers until Day 3 reveal stream)

**Days 3–14: Active Event**
- Collab banner live (SSR rate boosted to 5% for duration)
- Collab story chapter available (15-minute visual novel, optional)
- 3 event-specific daily missions (reward: collab-themed currency redeemable for non-banner cosmetics)
- Collab enemy variant: "Corrupted" versions of partner IP mechs serve as PvE raid targets
- Limited free collab cosmetic awarded at tier 5 of event mission track (accessible to all players who log in)

**Post-Event (Days 15–30): Afterglow**
- Non-banner collab cosmetics available in shop for Credits (no gacha; direct purchase)
- Collab story chapter archived and accessible permanently
- Collab cosmetics enter the "Collab Vault" — never re-released in standard gacha; only available in future collab anniversary events (once per year)

### Revenue Modeling

**Major Collab (Evangelion, Code Geass scale)**
- Active spenders during event: 600,000 (elevated from baseline 450,000 due to crossover audience)
- ARPPU during 14-day event: $35 (elevated by limited-time pressure)
- Per-event revenue: 600,000 × $35 = $21M
- Platform cut (Apple/Google 30%): $14.7M net
- 2 major collabs per year: **$29.4M net / ~$42M gross**

**Minor Collab (Gunbuster, SSSS scale)**
- Active spenders: 480,000
- ARPPU: $22
- Per-event revenue: $10.56M
- 2 minor collabs per year: **$14.8M net / ~$21M gross**

**Total annual collab revenue (gross): ~$63M base**

At higher-end assumptions (broader crossover audience, more aggressive banner pricing):
- 2 major collabs at $40M each = $80M
- 2 minor collabs at $15M each = $30M
- Total: **$110M gross**

### Partner Incentive Model

Revenue sharing: 70% Nexus / 30% partner IP holder

Partner IP holder receives:
- 30% of all banner revenue attributable to the collab (tracked by collab-specific currency purchase SKUs)
- Co-branded physical kit release (if applicable; negotiated per deal)
- In-game advertising credit equivalent (story chapter serves as branded content)
- Data report on player engagement with their IP (anonymized, aggregate)

Nexus retains 70% and all non-banner event revenue (direct shop sales, pass uplift attributable to the event window).

## Revenue Engine 5: PvP Tournament Pass

**Annual Revenue Potential: $60–100M**

### World Duel Championship Structure

The World Duel Championship (WDC) is Gundam Nexus's organized competitive ecosystem. It mirrors real-world fighting game tournament circuits but adapted to the Duel Arena Protocol (see Witch from Mercury system in COMBINATORIAL_SYSTEMS.md).

**Monthly Regional Brackets**
- 32-player single-elimination brackets per region (NA, EU, JP, KR, SEA, OCE, LATAM, MENA)
- Open entry: any player above Platinum rank
- Matches streamed on Nexus official Twitch/YouTube channels
- Top 8 per region qualify for Quarterly World Finals

**Quarterly World Finals**
- 64 players (8 regions × 8 qualifiers)
- Bracketed over a single weekend (Saturday: top 64 to top 8; Sunday: top 8 finals)
- Live event option: partnered esports venues host viewing events with cosplay, kit display, guest panels
- Prize pool (cosmetic-only): Champion receives exclusive gold-nameplate "World Duel Champion" suit skin displayed on their profile for the quarter; runner-up receives silver variant
- No cash prizes — avoids gambling regulation entanglement and ensures amateur-friendly competition

### Tournament Pass — $4.99/Month

The Tournament Pass is a low-friction subscription targeting the broad competitive player base, not just active tournament participants.

**What It Includes:**
- Spectator Mode enhanced view: follow-cam options, stat overlay, damage breakdown
- Live match notifications (push notifications when followed players or regional finals go live)
- Exclusive "Tournament Emblem" animated badge (series changes each quarter)
- "Champion's Chronicle" in-game log: tracks personal match history with video replay (auto-deleted at 30 days without pass)
- 1 Tournament Pilot suit cosmetic per quarter (pass-exclusive; never in gacha)
- Early bracket access: pass holders see bracket draws 24 hours before public announcement

**Streaming Integration (Viewer Drops)**
- Twitch and YouTube integration: viewers watching official Nexus broadcasts receive cosmetic drops (requires linked account)
- Drops include: rare emblems, beam color variants, "Spectator" cockpit badge
- Drop schedule: 1 drop per 30 minutes watched during official matches
- Drops serve as marketing (players who don't have the game see drops advertised and are converted)

### Esports Organization Licensing

Gundam Nexus sells official team slots to established esports organizations.

**Tier Structure:**
| Tier | Annual Fee | Benefits |
|------|------------|---------|
| Partner Org | $200,000/year | 2 reserved qualifier spots, team jersey cosmetics in-game for all accounts, coach spectator slot |
| Affiliate Org | $50,000/year | 1 reserved qualifier spot, team jersey cosmetics for org members, social media co-promotion |
| Community Team | $5,000/year | Community team page, custom team emblem in-game |

Year 1 target: 10 Partner Orgs + 20 Affiliate Orgs = $3M/year in org licensing
Year 3 target: 25 Partner Orgs + 50 Affiliate Orgs = $7.5M/year in org licensing

Team jersey cosmetics are purchasable by general players in the shop for 480 Credits each ($8). At 10 teams × 5,000 average sales per jersey set = $400K additional annual cosmetic revenue.

## Anti-Predatory Commitments and Regulatory Compliance

### Core Commitments

Gundam Nexus is built on the following monetization ethics, enshrined in the Terms of Service and enforced technically, not just aspirationally:

**1. No Gameplay Impact in Random Systems**
No item obtainable through a randomized (gacha) purchase affects match outcome, suit statistics, or game mechanics. All gacha items are cosmetic without exception. This commitment is permanently locked in the game's design; no future update can add stat-affecting gacha items without a full revision to the ESRB/PEGI rating and Terms of Service.

**2. Full Odds Transparency**
All randomized pull rates are displayed:
- On every banner purchase screen before credits are spent
- In the in-game "Probability Archive" accessible from main menu
- In the App Store / Play Store listing description
- On the official website's dedicated "Gacha Rates" page

**3. Monthly Spending Cap**
A hard monthly spending cap of $500 per account per calendar month is enforced at the payment processor level. This cap is:
- Not circumventable by creating secondary accounts (device fingerprinting and payment method matching are used)
- Adjustable downward by the account holder (minimum $0 — full self-exclusion is supported)
- Adjustable by verified parents for accounts of players under 18 (Family Mode, PIN-protected)

**4. No Energy System**
Gundam Nexus has no stamina, energy, or action-point system. Players may engage in as many matches and sessions as they choose without being gated by a resource that recovers on a timer. This eliminates a major predatory engagement pattern.

**5. All Suits Earnable Through Play**
Every mobile suit available in Gundam Nexus is earnable through gameplay without spending real money. Earning through play requires 200+ hours per suit for the rarest units — a long but achievable time investment for dedicated players. No suit is ever exclusively available via gacha.

The 200-hour figure is intentional: it is honest about the time commitment rather than artificially accessible. Players who prefer to purchase suits directly at flat prices ($4.99–$14.99) are supported and encouraged. The point is that neither path requires gacha spending.

### What Gundam Nexus Will Never Do

The following practices are permanently prohibited in the Nexus monetization model regardless of revenue pressure:

- Time-limited exclusive suits that can never be earned through play (FOMO traps)
- Gacha items that affect combat statistics, damage, defense, or speed
- "Energy drinks" or consumables that grant competitive advantages
- Player-vs.-player real-money wagering on match outcomes
- Resale or transfer of in-game items for real money (grey market prevention)
- Loot boxes presented as "surprise" purchases without pre-disclosed odds
- Paywalled ranked modes or competitive features

## Conclusion

The Gundam Nexus monetization model targets $2.5B over five years through five complementary revenue engines that serve different player segments. The model is designed to be player-first: cosmetic-only randomization, transparent odds, hard spending caps, and universal suit accessibility through play.

The defining asymmetry is that spending in Nexus is an expression of fandom and identity, not a path to competitive advantage. A player who spends $500 on gacha cosmetics and a player who spends $0 and grinds 300 hours are equally capable of winning matches. The monetization model only works sustainably if this remains true.

The Gunpla AR Bridge is the highest-ceiling and most differentiated element. No other live service game has a direct physical-product integration at this scale with a partner (Bandai Namco) who controls both the physical and digital rights to the franchise. If the AR Bridge achieves its Year 3 projections, it alone may represent the largest physical-digital product integration in gaming history.
